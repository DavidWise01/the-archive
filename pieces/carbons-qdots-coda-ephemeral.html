The Ephemeral Dot

a sandbox, not a fabrication

label: model — not physics, not a discovery

Its whole charm is that it never gets fabricated — just spun up and thrown away. A real dot pays the confinement tax: it gets discrete levels because being small forces them on it. An ephemeral dot pays nothing — and inherits nothing. So the sandbox doesn't hand you the principle for free. You have to write it in.

Read this first
  • This pamphlet describes a conceptual construct — a sandbox — not a physical device and not a discovery. It sits beside the real quantum-dot series as its imagined sibling.
  • A real dot's behavior is imposed by nature. A simulated dot's behavior is imposed by you — it only does what you code it to do.
  • The value here is freedom from hardware: explore a principle without a fab. The cost is that nothing inside counts as evidence about real dots unless you deliberately put it there.
  • Keep the label "sandbox," not "discovery," and everything below is clean fun.

The core of it
  • An ephemeral dot is a sandbox construct: instantiated on demand, never fabricated, gone when unrendered.
  • Its charm is freedom from hardware; its catch is that it inherits no physics — a real dot is forced into discrete levels by smallness, while a simulated one does only what you write in.
  • The −0.01 layer is a nesting index — a declaration that it lives one notch below your substrate — not a physical measurement.
  • A sim inside a sim is the turtle stack on purpose — the doorway to the simulation argument.
  • It's genuinely useful for exploring a principle unbound, but its results are about your model, not nature, unless real physics was deliberately coded in.
The honest footing
  • This is a model, stated as one. It is not a claim about physics and not a discovery — the rest of the quantum-dot series is the real, sourced story; this is its imagined coda.
  • "Same principle" is aspirational: a sandbox only embodies a principle you put into it. Don't mistake authored behavior for inherited behavior.
  • The −0.01 and the nesting are conceptual bookkeeping — evocative, not physical quantities. Treated as metaphor, they're fine; treated as measurements, they'd mislead.
  • Sandboxes are powerful and legitimate — most of modern science runs on them. The only rule is to keep the label straight.
