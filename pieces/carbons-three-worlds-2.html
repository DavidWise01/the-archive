THREE WORLDS
ONE FIGHTING ENGINE · THREE WAYS TO PLAY IT
a retro game-manual trilogy — conflict, resolution, judgment

Three little 8-bit "game manuals," built on a single timing-meter engine and then turned inside out three times. In FIST CITY you throw the punch. In PEACE CITY you refuse to. In SCORE CITY you stand in the middle and make the call.

Same chunky pixel art, same CRT glow, same beat-the-meter heart — three completely different things to be. Pick a world and read its files.

ONE ENGINE, THREE INVERSIONS
the same beat-the-meter loop, pointed three different directions
FIST · YOU ATTACK

Time the meter to strike. Drive their health bar to zero before yours runs out. A PERFECT lands a critical.

PEACE · YOU CALM

Same meter — but now you talk their tension down to zero while guarding your composure. A PERFECT is a breakthrough.

SCORE · YOU JUDGE

Same meter again — you officiate the match down to a clean finish while protecting your credibility. A PERFECT is a confirmed replay.

Each world also shares the deep stuff: a tiered ranking ladder, illustrated character files, five arcade modes (career, divisions, exhibition, a no-stakes practice, and an 8-seat tournament), difficulty and best-of-3, plus a wholesome juniors division and friendly cup with medals for everyone and no sore losers.
THREE WORLDS is an original parody trilogy. All styles, disciplines, officials, champions, records, and opponents across all three games are fictional and affectionate.
Press Space or Enter inside any world for its action button — Strike, Respond, or Make the Call.
