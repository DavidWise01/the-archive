The center

anchor · coordination 12

One node, bonded to all twelve. The only one that is. It carries no surface, sits at the origin, and is the reference every shell address is measured from. Remove it and the twelve have no center to be a shell of.

The twelve

shell · 3 squares of 4

Each shell node touches 4 siblings + the center = 5 bonds. None touches all — only the center does. The twelve fall into three mutually perpendicular squares (the x, y, z planes): the shell is three rings, not one ring of twelve.

The Thirteen — the FCC magic-number cluster Au₁₃, drawn as a cuboctahedron (1 core + 12 nearest neighbors, coordination 12). Real geometry: 12 vertices, 24 edges, 14 faces (8 triangular + 6 square), full octahedral symmetry Oh (order 48), with center-to-vertex distance equal to vertex-to-vertex edge — the touching-spheres condition. Honest note: Au₁₃ has two competing magic isomers — this cuboctahedron (FCC fragment, Oh) and the icosahedron (Ih, order 120); both are 1 + 12. Node roles are an assignment, not a derivation.
